Here is a compact digest of new or updated IETF drafts, W3C proposals, and standards-body activity on AI agent authentication, web bot auth, agents.txt, llms.txt, or agent identity from the last 30 days:

1.  **IETF Draft: AI Agent Authentication and Authorization (draft-klrc-aiagent-auth-01)**
    This IETF Internet-Draft was updated on March 30, 2026. It proposes a model for authenticating and authorizing AI agent interactions by leveraging existing standards such as the Workload Identity in Multi-System Environments (WIMSE) architecture and the OAuth 2.0 family of specifications, rather than defining new protocols. The document aims to provide a framework for applying and extending current standards to address AI agent authentication and authorization, identifying gaps for future standardization efforts.
    Authoritative Source: [https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-klrc-aiagent-auth-01/](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-klrc-aiagent-auth-01/)

2.  **IETF Draft: Web Bot Auth (WBA) for Cryptographic Agent Identity**
    Web Bot Auth (WBA) is an emerging IETF draft standard designed to provide a secure and verifiable identity layer for AI agents and automated bots. It enables these automated clients to cryptographically sign their HTTP requests using asymmetric key pairs, allowing websites to distinguish legitimate AI agents from malicious bots. Google is actively experimenting with Web Bot Auth, and Cloudflare is an active contributor to this draft.
    Authoritative Source: [https://nohacks.io/blog/google-agent-web-bot-auth/](https://nohacks.io/blog/google-agent-web-bot-auth/) (referencing Google's experimentation) and [https://www.cloudflare.com/blog/agent-readiness-score/](https://www.cloudflare.com/blog/agent-readiness-score/) (referencing Cloudflare's contribution).

3.  **IETF Draft: Agent Identity Registry System (draft-drake-agent-identity-registry-00)**
    Published on April 11, 2026, this IETF Internet-Draft proposes a federated architecture for hardware-anchored identity of autonomous entities. The system aims to issue standard OpenID Connect (OIDC) tokens, providing a robust identity infrastructure for AI agents, from cloud software to embodied robots.
    Authoritative Source: [https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-drake-agent-identity-registry-00/](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-drake-agent-identity-registry-00/)

4.  **IETF Draft: Agent Public Key Infrastructure (APKI) (draft-sharif-apki-agent-pki-00)**
    This IETF Internet-Draft, published on April 10, 2026, defines an Agent Public Key Infrastructure (APKI) for certificate-based identity and trust for autonomous AI agents. It addresses the limitations of traditional PKI (X.509 certificates) which were not designed for the ephemeral lifecycles, graduated trust scoring, and capability constraints characteristic of AI agents.
    Authoritative Source: [https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-sharif-apki-agent-pki-00/](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-sharif-apki-agent-pki-00/)

5.  **IETF Draft: Transaction Tokens For Agents (draft-oauth-transaction-tokens-for-agents-06)**
    Updated on April 11, 2026, this IETF Internet-Draft specifies an extension to OAuth Transaction Tokens to support agent context propagation within agent-based workloads. It defines the use of the 'act' field to identify the agent performing an action and leverages the existing 'sub' field for the principal, enabling more granular access control decisions and enhancing security.
    Authoritative Source: [https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-oauth-transaction-tokens-for-agents-06/](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-oauth-transaction-tokens-for-agents-06/)

6.  **W3C AI Agent Protocol Community Group Activity**
    The W3C AI Agent Protocol Community Group is actively working on standardizing inter-agent communication protocols and agent identity models to support secure and interoperable authentication between agents across the Web. A white paper on the Agent Network Protocol, published by this group on April 1, 2026, emphasizes the need for unified identity representation and verification mechanisms for agents to establish trusted identity claims and authentication across different platforms.
    Authoritative Source: [https://www.w3.org/community/ai-agent-protocol/](https://www.w3.org/community/ai-agent-protocol/) and [https://agent-network-protocol.github.io/whitepaper/](https://agent-network-protocol.github.io/whitepaper/)

7.  **Expiration of IETF agents.txt Draft**
    The `draft-agarwal-agents-txt` IETF draft, intended as a `robots.txt` equivalent for AI agents to declare supported capabilities, expired on April 10, 2026, without a successor. This event highlights the ongoing fragmentation in agent discovery mechanisms, with various competing approaches and a lack of a common discovery layer.
    Authoritative Source: [https://www.reddit.com/r/mcp/comments/1c0152f/agentstxt_ietf_draft_expires_tomorrow_what_does/](https://www.reddit.com/r/mcp/comments/1c0152f/agentstxt_ietf_draft_expires_tomorrow_what_does/)

8.  **Continued Discussion and Adoption of llms.txt (Proposed Standard)**
    Throughout March and April 2026, there has been extensive discussion and growing adoption of `llms.txt`, a proposed standard for websites to communicate structured content to Large Language Models (LLMs) and AI systems. While not yet an officially ratified standard by the W3C or IETF, it is gaining traction as a de facto standard, with many articles detailing its benefits, format, and how to implement it. Major AI platforms have not publicly confirmed its use, but its adoption by developers and SEO practitioners is rapidly increasing.
    Authoritative Source: [https://llmstxt.org/](https://llmstxt.org/) (the proposed standard's website) and various industry articles like [https://www.ahrefs.com/blog/llms-txt](https://www.ahrefs.com/blog/llms-txt) and [https://www.citeme.com/blog/llms-txt-file](https://www.citeme.com/blog/llms-txt-file).

9.  **NIST's AI Agent Standards Initiative**
    NIST launched its AI Agent Standards Initiative, marking a significant step in addressing AI agent identity and authorization for enterprise security. This initiative formally acknowledges the need for clear machine identity and robust governance mechanisms for AI agents, moving beyond traditional static policies to manage autonomous digital actors effectively.
    Authoritative Source: [https://www.techradar.com/pro/security/why-nists-ai-agent-standards-initiative-is-a-turning-point-for-enterprise-security](https://www.techradar.com/pro/security/why-nists-ai-agent-standards-initiative-is-a-turning-point-for-enterprise-security)
