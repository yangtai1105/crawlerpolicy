Here's a compact digest of the most important distinct items concerning AI agent authentication, web bot authentication, and agent identity from the last 30 days (March 20, 2026, to April 19, 2026):

1.  **IETF Draft Update: AI Agent Authentication and Authorization**
    An updated Internet-Draft, "AI Agent Authentication and Authorization" (draft-klrc-aiagent-auth-01), was published by the IETF on March 30, 2026. This document proposes a model for authenticating and authorizing AI agent interactions by leveraging existing standards like Workload Identity in Multi-System Environments (WIMSE) and OAuth 2.0, aiming to provide a framework for current standards and identify areas for future standardization.

2.  **New IETF Draft: Agent Identity Protocol (AIP)**
    A new Internet-Draft titled "Agent Identity Protocol (AIP): Decentralized Identity and Delegation for AI Agents" (draft-singla-agent-identity-protocol-00) was published on April 17, 2026. AIP defines a decentralized identity, delegation, and authorization framework for autonomous AI agents, combining W3C Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs), capability-based authorization, and cryptographic delegation chains for secure, auditable multi-agent workflows without relying on centralized identity providers.

3.  **IETF Activity on Discovery of Agents, Workloads, and Named Entities (DAWN)**
    The IETF has published new requirements for how AI agents can discover, verify, and trust each other across organizational boundaries, referred to as DAWN (Discovery of Agents, Workloads, and Named Entities), with an article discussing this development published on April 12, 2026. Key requirements include explicit capability declarations, policy-driven verification, and zero-knowledge attestation, emphasizing operation across trust boundaries without a single global trust anchor.

4.  **NIST AI Agent Standards Initiative Progress and Human-Anchored Intent-Bound Delegation (HAID)**
    The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) continued its AI Agent Standards Initiative, with a deadline for submitting input on the "Accelerating the Adoption of Software and AI Agent Identity and Authorization Concept Paper" by April 2, 2026. Additionally, a framework called Human-Anchored Intent-Bound Delegation (HAID) was proposed on April 2, 2026, in response to NIST's concept paper, aiming to bind agent permissions to human-declared intent to prevent prompt injection and enable accountability. The draft on automated benchmark evaluations also closed on March 31, 2026.

5.  **Evolution and Expiration of `agents.txt` Proposals**
    The proposed web standard for AI agent interaction declarations, initially known as `agents.txt`, underwent renaming to `agent-manifest.txt` in March 2026, and subsequently to `agents-brief.txt` in early April 2026. Concurrently, an IETF draft related to `agents.txt` (draft-agarwal-agents-txt) expired on April 10, 2026, without being adopted by a working group, highlighting ongoing fragmentation in agent discovery efforts. Separately, a project promoting an `agents.txt` standard for AI agent discovery, distinct from the IETF draft, has shown recent activity.

6.  **Continued Discussion and Adoption of `llms.txt` and `llms-full.txt`**
    Discussions and updates regarding `llms.txt` and its companion `llms-full.txt` as a method for websites to provide AI-friendly content continued into April 2026. These files are designed to offer AI systems a curated map or full text of a site's most important content, aiming to improve AI visibility and understanding, particularly for developer-focused documentation.

7.  **Web Bot Auth Gaining Traction as an Emerging IETF Standard**
    Web Bot Auth is an emerging open standard progressing through the IETF, designed to enable automated clients to cryptographically sign their HTTP requests, thereby allowing bots to prove their identity with high confidence. An article published on March 24, 2026, detailed how Web Bot Auth works and provided a testing page for implementations, noting its adoption by major platforms like Cloudflare, AWS, and Akamai.
