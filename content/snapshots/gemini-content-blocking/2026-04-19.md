Here's a compact digest of the most important distinct developments for publishers to block, charge, or control AI crawlers from the last 14 days (April 5, 2026, to April 19, 2026):

1.  **News Outlets Block Wayback Machine to Prevent AI Training**
    A growing number of news organizations are restricting the Internet Archive's Wayback Machine from crawling and preserving their content. This action stems from concerns that AI companies might circumvent direct AI crawler blocks by accessing archived versions of their pages for model training, thereby using their content without permission or compensation. At least 23 major news sites are reportedly blocking the `ia-archiverbot`.

2.  **Cloudflare Introduces New AI Crawl Control Features**
    Cloudflare has rolled out new features to help publishers manage AI crawler interactions. These include "Redirects for AI Training," which allows publishers to enforce canonical content for verified crawlers, and an "Agent Readiness score" to help site owners understand how well their websites support AI agents. These additions build upon Cloudflare's existing "Pay Per Crawl" private beta, which enables publishers to charge AI companies for content access, and its "AI Crawl Control" for granular management of crawler behavior.

3.  **Arc XP Integrates TollBit for Mid-Size Publishers to Monetize AI Access**
    In a significant development for mid-size publishers, Arc XP has integrated TollBit, a platform designed to help content creators monetize access for AI crawlers. This integration provides a mechanism for publishers who may not have the resources to negotiate direct licensing deals with AI companies, allowing them to charge AI bots for content access. The Philadelphia Inquirer, for example, plans to adopt TollBit through this integration.
