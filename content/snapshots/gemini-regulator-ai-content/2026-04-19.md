Here is a compact digest of important regulatory developments from the last 30 days concerning AI training data, AI crawlers, content licensing, and generative AI and copyright:

1.  **European Parliament Calls for Stronger Copyright Framework for Generative AI**
    On March 10, 2026, the European Parliament adopted a Resolution on Copyright and Generative Artificial Intelligence, signaling a potential shift in European copyright policy. The resolution, building on a report from the Committee on Legal Affairs published on February 25, 2026, advocates for clarifying and strengthening the EU copyright framework, ensuring creators retain control and fair remuneration for their works used in AI training. It also suggests that EU copyright law should apply to generative AI models available in the EU, regardless of where the training takes place.

2.  **UK Government Abandons Plans for Broad AI Copyright Exception**
    The UK government has reversed its earlier inclination to allow AI companies to freely access copyrighted material for training purposes by default. This shift, announced around March 19, 2026, follows significant feedback from creative industries. The government's "Report on Copyright and Artificial Intelligence," published on March 18, 2026, confirms that a broad copyright exception for text and data mining (TDM) with an opt-out is no longer its preferred approach. Instead, the UK will now encourage industry-led licensing arrangements and monitor global developments before considering legislative changes.

3.  **Italian Court Annuls Garante's €15 Million Fine Against OpenAI**
    On March 18, 2026, the Court of Rome annulled the Italian Data Protection Authority (Garante per la Protezione dei Dati Personali)'s €15 million fine against OpenAI. The fine, issued on November 2, 2024, was for multiple GDPR violations related to the management of its ChatGPT service, including processing personal data for training without an adequate legal basis and failing to inform users adequately. This ruling overturns what was, at the time, the only final GDPR enforcement action in Europe concerning generative AI.
