## News

[OpenAI has introduced Workspace Agents in ChatGPT](https://openai.com/index/introducing-workspace-agents-in-chatgpt), a new capability that leverages Codex to automate complex workflows in a cloud-based environment. The agents are designed to help teams scale work across multiple tools while maintaining security controls. This announcement builds on OpenAI's recent enterprise AI push, including the [Agents SDK with sandbox execution](https://openai.com/blog/agents-sdk-sandbox) and [training programs for ChatGPT workflow automation](https://openai.com/index/openai-academy-workspace-agents).

## Why it matters

Workspace Agents represent a direct escalation of OpenAI's agentic infrastructure for enterprise deployment. The emphasis on cloud-native, team-scale automation signals OpenAI's intent to compete directly in the business-process-automation space—territory traditionally occupied by RPA vendors and orchestration platforms. This is the third major agent-related announcement in a single month (following the Agents SDK hardening on 2026-04-15 and the enterprise integration suite on 2026-04-08), indicating aggressive product velocity. For publishers and SaaS vendors, the integration patterns (cross-tool, team-managed, security-conscious) suggest OpenAI is building the control-plane layer that will make agents sticky in enterprise environments. Regulators may scrutinize the security model and data residency implications as agents gain permission to act on behalf of teams across multiple applications.
