## Implication

This expansion signals a maturing ecosystem: the addition of DAWN and HAID indicates the standards community is moving beyond authentication toward trust verification, cross-organizational discovery, and human-centered accountability—addressing prompt-injection and delegation governance gaps that single-protocol approaches (WIMSE/OAuth, AIP) cannot solve alone. The inclusion of Web Bot Auth adoption by major CDNs signals operational readiness and may accelerate IETF progression. However, the concurrent expiration of draft-agarwal-agents-txt and proliferation of rename cycles in agent-discovery manifests (`agents-brief.txt` now distinct from community efforts) reveals unresolved fragmentation: practitioners now face a choice between IETF-track standards (WIMSE, Web Bot Auth, DAWN), decentralized identity protocols (AIP), and web manifests (multiple naming schemes)—a sign the ecosystem has not yet converged on a primary discovery or delegation standard. The rise of intent-bound and human-anchored frameworks (HAID) also marks a shift from pure cryptographic identity toward governance-aware models, a third axis beyond authentication and discovery. For policy and standards bodies, this moment reflects healthy competition but risks creating implementation sprawl unless coordination intensifies in coming months.

## What changed

The digest shifted from a narrow focus on five early IETF and W3C items to a comprehensive seven-point inventory spanning March–April 2026. New material added: (1) [DAWN (Discovery of Agents, Workloads, and Named Entities)](https://www.moltbook.com/post/58e8ea09-bcb2-41c7-8400-3669695c8030) with explicit capability declarations and zero-knowledge attestation; (2) [NIST AI Agent Standards Initiative progress and the Human-Anchored Intent-Bound Delegation (HAID) framework](https://www.thefai.org/posts/human-anchored-intent-bound-delegation-for-ai-agents), binding agent permissions to human intent to prevent prompt injection; (3) [Web Bot Auth](https://fingerprint.com/blog/web-bot-auth-guide/) as an emerging IETF standard with adoption by Cloudflare, AWS, and Akamai. The digest also expanded detail on AIP (now citing v-00, with specifics on DIDs and cryptographic delegation chains) and documented the fragmentation in agent-discovery naming (`agents.txt` → `agent-manifest.txt` → `agents-brief.txt`, with draft-agarwal-agents-txt expiring on April 10).
