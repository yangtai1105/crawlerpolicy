## What changed

A new, full-length article appeared in DataDome's feed covering agentic commerce in the ticketing industry. The piece defines agentic commerce (AI agents autonomously browsing, comparing, and transacting on behalf of users), explains why ticketing is a prime use case, and outlines both platform opportunities (reduced cart abandonment, improved fan accessibility, agent-first infrastructure) and five specific threat/challenge categories: DDoS-like agent traffic floods, new fraud vectors from pre-authorized spending authority, scalpers disguising bots as legitimate agents, loss of upsell/cross-sell revenue, and disappearing behavioral data. DataDome positions its Priority Protect and Agent Trust Management products as the solution layer. The article cites industry projections that agentic commerce could account for 37% of UK ticket sales by 2028, and references emerging payment protocols from Visa (Trusted Agent Protocol) and Mastercard (Agent Pay).

## Implication

This piece signals that DataDome is actively framing "agent trust management" as a distinct product category, separate from traditional bot management—publishers and platform operators should note the shift in vendor positioning. The identification of scalpers wrapping bots in legitimate agentic commerce identities (valid API credentials, believable user-agent strings) is a concrete new threat model that security and fraud teams in ticketing and high-demand e-commerce need to evaluate now. The tension between blocking malicious agents and allowing legitimate consumer AI agents is a meaningful operational challenge: over-blocking risks losing a projected one-third of future transactions. The article also surfaces a liability gap—unclear responsibility when an AI agent overpays or makes unwanted purchases—which has policy and legal implications for platforms that enable agentic checkout. For agent-infrastructure practitioners, the lack of granular spending controls in current payment protocols (Visa, Mastercard) is a notable gap worth tracking as standards evolve.
