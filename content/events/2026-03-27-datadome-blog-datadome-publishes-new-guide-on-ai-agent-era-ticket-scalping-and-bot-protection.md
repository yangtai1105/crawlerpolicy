## What changed

DataDome's blog feed gained a full-length article covering ticket scalping bots and their intersection with AI agent threats. The piece introduces DataDome's "Priority Protect" virtual waiting room product and "Account Protect" as defensive layers. Notably, the article explicitly frames malicious AI agents—not just traditional bots—as an emerging threat vector for scalping, citing Gartner's prediction that 20% of digital interactions will involve AI agents by 2028. It also references DataDome's own "Global Bot Security Report 2025," claiming 61.2% of domains are unprotected against simple bot attacks and advanced bots evade detection in 93% of cases. A real-world attack case study is included: 16 million malicious requests from nearly 4 million unique IPs targeting sporting event tickets.

## Implication

For publisher and platform operators in ticketing, travel, and e-commerce, this article signals that major bot-detection vendors are now explicitly positioning AI agent identity verification—not just bot fingerprinting—as a required capability. The framing of "agent trust management" as distinct from traditional bot management reflects a market shift practitioners should track when evaluating vendor roadmaps. The cited statistics (61.2% unprotected domains, 93% advanced bot evasion rate) are vendor-sourced and should be treated as marketing claims, but they indicate the benchmarks DataDome is setting for the category. Policy and infrastructure teams building queue or checkout systems should note the emphasis on continuous in-session analysis rather than entry-point checks alone. The explicit mention of LLM crawler visibility and per-agent policy controls suggests bot-management APIs will increasingly need to surface agent-type metadata to downstream enforcement logic.
